Cookies & Supercookies

Small text files stored on your computer that track your browsing history and preferences across sessions.

How They Work

Standard cookies are text files that websites store on your device to remember information about you:

  • Session cookies track your activity during a browser session
  • Persistent cookies remain after you close your browser
  • Third-party cookies come from domains other than the one you're visiting

Supercookies are more invasive tracking mechanisms:

  • HSTS supercookies abuse web security features
  • Evercookies use multiple storage techniques for persistence
  • ETags exploit browser caching mechanisms
Protection

Use browser extensions like "Cookie AutoDelete" or "Privacy Badger". Configure your browser to delete cookies when you close it, and regularly clear your browser data.

Browser Fingerprinting

Creating a unique identifier based on your browser and device characteristics, without using cookies.

How It Works

Websites collect information about your browser setup to create a unique identifier:

  • Installed plugins and their versions
  • Browser type, version, and settings
  • Operating system and hardware details
  • Screen resolution and color depth
  • Installed fonts
  • Language preferences
  • Time zone information
  • Canvas fingerprinting (using HTML5 canvas element)
  • WebGL fingerprinting (GPU information)
  • Audio fingerprinting

The combination of these elements creates a fingerprint that's highly unique.

Protection

Use privacy-focused browsers like Firefox with fingerprinting protection, Brave, or Tor. Add extensions like "Canvas Blocker" or "Privacy Badger" and disable JavaScript when possible.

Tracking Pixels

Invisible 1x1 pixel images embedded in websites and emails that track when you view content.

How They Work

Tracking pixels are tiny, invisible images (usually 1x1 pixel) embedded in:

  • Websites
  • Emails
  • Digital advertisements
  • Social media posts

When loaded, they send information to a server:

  • User IP address
  • Time and duration of visit
  • Operating system
  • Email open rates (in email marketing)
  • Device type

Common examples include Facebook Pixel, Google Analytics pixel, and LinkedIn Insight Tag.

Protection

Use ad blockers like uBlock Origin, disable automatic image loading in email clients, and consider email privacy tools like SimpleLogin or AnonAddy.

Local Storage & Session Storage

Browser-based storage mechanisms that store larger amounts of data than cookies and can persist across sessions.

How They Work

Web Storage API provides two mechanisms for storing data client-side:

  • localStorage: Persists even after browser is closed (no expiration date)
  • sessionStorage: Only available for the duration of the page session

Compared to cookies, these methods:

  • Store significantly more data (5MB vs 4KB for cookies)
  • Never sent to the server automatically
  • Can only be accessed from the same domain

Used for both legitimate purposes (saving user preferences) and tracking (storing user identifiers).

Protection

Regularly clear browser data, use private browsing, or extensions like "Cookie AutoDelete" (which can also clear local storage). Some privacy-focused browsers include automatic clearing options.

E-commerce & Social Media Tracking

Shopping sites and social networks track your product views, clicks, and interests to create comprehensive profiles.

How It Works

E-commerce and social media platforms utilize multiple techniques:

  • Product/post engagement tracking
  • Cart abandonment tracking
  • Purchase history analysis
  • Cross-site tracking via social login buttons
  • Interest-based profiling
  • Location-based recommendations
  • Device correlation (matching your identity across devices)

This data builds comprehensive profiles used for targeted advertising and behavioral prediction.

Protection

Use dedicated shopping browsers, disable social login options, and consider tools like "Facebook Container" extension. Log out of social accounts when browsing the web.

Cross-Site Tracking & CNAME Cloaking

Advanced techniques that follow you across different websites and disguise third-party trackers as first-party resources.

How It Works

Cross-Site Tracking:

  • Tracking scripts embedded on multiple websites
  • Social media sharing buttons monitoring site visits
  • Ad networks correlating activity across their network
  • Single Sign-On services monitoring logins

CNAME Cloaking:

  • Uses DNS CNAME records to make third-party trackers appear as first-party
  • Bypasses many tracker blockers and browser protections
  • Makes tracking domains look like they belong to the website you're visiting
  • Increasingly common as standard third-party cookie blocking improves
Protection

Use browsers with anti-tracking protection (Firefox, Brave), DNS-level blocking (Pi-hole, NextDNS), and extensions like uBlock Origin with CNAME-uncloaking capabilities.

What Data Is Collected

The extent of information websites gather about you is both vast and concerning.

Personal Identifiers

Your name, email, phone number, address, and unique IDs assigned by tracking services. Even if you don't explicitly provide this information, sites can often associate your browsing with your identity through login status or cross-site tracking.

Technical Data

Your IP address, device information, browser type, operating system, screen resolution, connection speed, and mobile carrier. This data helps create your unique browser fingerprint and can reveal your approximate location.

Behavioral Data

Every click, hover, scroll pattern, mouse movements, time spent on page, and forms you fill out. This data is used to analyze your engagement, interests, and determine how likely you are to convert or make a purchase.

Location Data

Your precise location (if you grant permission) or approximate location based on IP address. This information is used for geo-targeting content, location-based services, and regional compliance purposes.

Preference & Interest Data

Your search history, content interests, preferences, purchase history, and affinities. Sites build detailed profiles about what you like, what you might buy, and what content keeps you engaged the longest.

Social Media & Connection Data

When you use social login or share content, sites collect social connections, friend lists, profile data and activity. This enriches their user profiles and reveals your social graph.

Sensitive Data

Some sites collect health information, financial data, sexual preferences, political views, and other sensitive details that can be used for highly targeted advertising or potentially discriminatory practices.

Protecting Yourself

Practical steps you can take to minimize tracking and protect your online privacy.

Browser Protection

Configure your browser settings and extensions to minimize tracking.

Recommended Tools

Privacy-Focused Browsers:

  • Firefox with privacy settings enhanced
  • Brave Browser
  • Tor Browser (maximum anonymity)

Essential Extensions:

  • uBlock Origin - Comprehensive ad and tracker blocking
  • Privacy Badger - Learns to block invisible trackers
  • HTTPS Everywhere - Forces encrypted connections
  • Decentraleyes - Protects against CDN tracking
  • Canvas Blocker - Prevents fingerprinting

Browser Settings:

  • Disable third-party cookies
  • Enable tracking protection features
  • Clear cookies and site data when closing
  • Use a private/incognito window for sensitive browsing

Network-Level Protection

Block tracking at the network level before it reaches any of your devices.

Recommended Tools

DNS-Level Blocking:

  • Pi-hole - Network-wide ad and tracker blocking
  • NextDNS - Cloud-based DNS filtering
  • AdGuard Home - Similar to Pi-hole with additional features

VPN Services:

  • Use a no-logs VPN service to mask your IP address
  • Look for VPNs with built-in tracker blocking features
  • Consider multi-hop VPN connections for sensitive activities

Advanced Techniques:

  • Tor network for maximum anonymity
  • Custom router firmware with advanced security features
  • Network traffic analysis tools to identify unwanted connections

Remember

No solution provides 100% protection against all tracking methods. The key is to use multiple layers of protection and be conscious about what information you share online. Regularly update your privacy tools, as tracking technologies constantly evolve.
