Understanding Stylometry

The statistical analysis of writing style to determine authorship

What is Stylometry?

Stylometry analyzes writing style to identify authors based on their unique linguistic "fingerprints"

Author A

The morning sun cast long shadows across the deserted street. He paused, lighting a cigarette, observing the quiet scene. It was always like this—peaceful, predictable, almost too perfect.

Author B

Sunlight flooded the empty avenue at dawn. She stopped and watched, taking in the stillness and silence. This place—it never changed, remained constantly serene, eerily flawless.

Key Metrics in Stylometry

Word Length

Average word length and distribution of word sizes

Sentence Length

Average sentences per paragraph and words per sentence

Vocabulary Richness

Type-token ratio and lexical diversity

Function Words

Usage patterns of articles, pronouns, and prepositions

How Algorithms Analyze Text

The old house creaked under the weight of years. She walked slowly through the rooms, remembering childhood summers and winter evenings by the fireplace. Every corner held a memory, every shadow a story. It was strange how time could transform a place yet leave it fundamentally unchanged.

The Analysis Process:

1. Text Preprocessing

Clean text by removing punctuation, converting to lowercase, and tokenizing into words and sentences

2. Feature Extraction

Identify and measure linguistic features like word frequencies, sentence structures, and grammatical patterns

3. Statistical Analysis

Apply statistical methods to quantify stylistic differences and similarities between texts

4. Machine Learning Classification

Train algorithms to recognize patterns and classify texts based on author-specific features

Author Fingerprinting

Every author has a unique "fingerprint" based on their writing style

Individual vs. Group

Algorithms can distinguish between individual authors and detect group writing patterns

Evolution Over Time

Author styles can evolve, but core stylistic elements often remain detectable

Cross-Genre Analysis

Stylometric patterns persist even when authors write across different genres

Confidence Levels

Modern algorithms provide statistical confidence measures for authorship attribution

Real-World Applications

Literary Analysis

Identifying anonymous authors of historical texts and resolving disputed attributions

Example: Determining the true authors of The Federalist Papers

Forensic Linguistics

Analyzing threatening letters, ransom notes, and suspicious communications

Example: Identifying authors of anonymous threatening messages

Plagiarism Detection

Identifying writing style inconsistencies that may indicate plagiarism

Example: Detecting ghostwritten academic papers or books

Online Security

Authenticating user identities through writing style analysis

Example: Identifying fake accounts or impersonators on social media
