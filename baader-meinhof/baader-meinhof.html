The Baader-Meinhof Phenomenon

Have you ever learned a new word or concept, like serendipity, and then suddenly started noticing it everywhere? This is often attributed to the Baader-Meinhof phenomenon, also known as the frequency illusion. It's not that instances of serendipity have actually increased; rather, your brain is now primed to notice it. The feeling of serendipity itself can sometimes feel like this phenomenon.

Once you become aware of something, like the concept of serendipity, your attention is drawn to it more easily. It's a fascinating cognitive bias that makes us believe things are happening more frequently than they are. The joy of an unexpected discovery, that moment of serendipity, can be quite powerful. You might find serendipity in books, conversations, or even in the most mundane situations. It's all about perception and how our awareness of serendipity shifts.

After learning about it, you might find the idea of serendipity popping up in unexpected places, almost by serendipity.
