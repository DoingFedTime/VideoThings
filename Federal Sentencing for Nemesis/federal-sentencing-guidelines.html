Federal Sentencing Guidelines

Case Study: Nemesis Darknet Market

Case Background

Behrouz Parsarad, an Iranian national, was the sole administrator of Nemesis Market, a darknet marketplace operating on the Tor network between 2021-2024. The market facilitated over 400,000 orders, had 150,000+ registered users and 1,100+ vendors, with transactions exceeding $30 million. Parsarad has been charged with conspiracy to distribute narcotics, conspiracy to launder money, and six counts of drug distribution.

Base Offense Levels

Conspiracy to Distribute Narcotics +26

Base offense level for drug conspiracy under USSG §2D1.1

Legal Citation: United States Sentencing Guidelines (USSG) §2D1.1 - Unlawful Manufacturing, Importing, Exporting, or Trafficking

Fentanyl Distribution +6 to +12

Additional levels based on the presence of fentanyl in the drug conspiracy, elevating level to 32-38

Legal Citation: USSG §2D1.1(c) - Drug Quantity Table with specific enhancements for fentanyl under §2D1.1(b)(13)

Money Laundering Conspiracy +8

Base offense level for money laundering conspiracy under USSG §2S1.1

Legal Citation: USSG §2S1.1 - Laundering of Monetary Instruments

Amount Laundered Enhancement +16

Specific offense characteristic for laundering more than $1.5 million (Treasury confirmed over $1.6 million)

Legal Citation: USSG §2S1.1(b)(2) referencing §2B1.1(b)(1) - Theft, Property Destruction, and Fraud

Distribution Counts +6 to +10

Six individual counts of narcotics distribution, either grouped or calculated separately

Legal Citation: USSG §3D1.2 - Groups of Closely Related Counts and §3D1.4 - Determining the Combined Offense Level

Upward Departures & Enhancements

Leadership Role +4

Organizer or leader of criminal activity involving five or more participants

Legal Citation: USSG §3B1.1(a) - Aggravating Role

Sophisticated Means +2

Use of complex or sophisticated technology to evade detection (Tor routing, Monero transactions, built-in mixers)

Legal Citation: USSG §2S1.1(b)(3) and §2B1.1(b)(10)(C) - Sophisticated Means

Obstruction of Justice +2

Warning users to "clean house" after takedown

Legal Citation: USSG §3C1.1 - Obstructing or Impeding the Administration of Justice

Special Skills +2

Use of specialized technical skills to build and maintain the marketplace infrastructure

Legal Citation: USSG §3B1.3 - Abuse of Position of Trust or Use of Special Skill

International Distribution +2

Cross-border transactions involving vendors in multiple countries (Germany, US, etc.)

Legal Citation: USSG §2D1.1(b)(5) - Importation of Controlled Substances

Criminal Livelihood +2

Primary income derived from criminal activity over several years

Legal Citation: USSG §2D1.1(b)(16)(E) and §4B1.3 - Criminal Livelihood

Total Sentencing Calculation

  • Base Offense Level: 26 (Drug Conspiracy)
  • Fentanyl Enhancement: +6 to +12 (= Level 32-38)
  • Money Laundering: Level 24 (8 base + 16 amount enhancement)
  • Distribution Counts: +6 to +10
  • Role/Criminal History Enhancements: +14 (4+2+2+2+2+2)

Final Offense Level: 43+ (Maximum Sentencing Table Level)

Recommended Sentence: Life Imprisonment under USSG Ch.5, Pt.A, Sentencing Table

Additional Sentencing Considerations

The Department of Justice may argue for additional variances based on national security implications or cyber-enabled criminal threats, which could potentially result in sentences beyond the standard guidelines.

The court retains discretion in sentencing, but given the multiple enhancements and the severity of the charges, the guidelines strongly recommend a life sentence in this case.
