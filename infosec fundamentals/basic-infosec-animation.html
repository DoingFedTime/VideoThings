Information Security (InfoSec) is the practice of protecting information by mitigating information risks. It encompasses preventing unauthorized access, use, disclosure, disruption, modification, or destruction of data.

The cornerstone of InfoSec is the CIA triad: Confidentiality (ensuring information is accessible only to authorized users), Integrity (maintaining accuracy and completeness of data), and Availability (ensuring information is accessible when needed).

The InfoSec threat landscape includes malware, phishing, social engineering, insider threats, denial-of-service attacks, and advanced persistent threats (APTs) from state actors, cybercriminals, and hacktivists.

Technical Controls

üíª

Technical security controls include firewalls, intrusion detection systems, encryption, multi-factor authentication, and endpoint protection that create technical barriers against threats.

Data Protection

üíæ

Data protection involves securing both data at rest (stored data), data in transit (moving across networks), and data in use (active data being processed) through encryption, access controls, and data loss prevention strategies.

Network Security

üåê

Network security focuses on protecting the integrity, confidentiality, and accessibility of computer networks through segmentation, monitoring, and implementing secure protocols.

Identity & Access Management

üë§

IAM ensures the right individuals access the right resources at the right times for the right reasons through authentication (verifying identity) and authorization (determining access rights).

Cryptography

üîë

Cryptography uses mathematical algorithms to encode information, enabling secure communication and data protection through encryption, digital signatures, and secure key management.

Security Policies & Governance

üìú

Security policies, standards, and procedures form the foundation of an organization's security program, defining acceptable use, incident response, and compliance requirements.
